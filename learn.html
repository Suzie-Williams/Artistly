Learn more about the Principles of Design in Arts

Contrast
When an artist is using contrast, they are creating a difference between the elements in the piece to draw the viewers' attention to the contrasting elements. Contrast can be achieved by using contrast colors, sizes, and/or shapes.
Balance
Balance in a work of art creates harmony between the elements of art in that piece. Objects are in proper proportion, colors, and arrangement.
Movement
This is when artists use techniques to guide the viewers eyes through the image. It can be achieved by lines, proper use of perspectives, horizons, edges, patterns, flow in a direction.
Pattern
Is the uniform repetition of elements, where artists use the same colors, shapes, sizes and/or lines. Spirals and grids are examples of patterns. Patterns can be found in Zentangle, wallpapers, clothing in a drawing.
Emphasis
Is when an art creates a visually dominant area in the art piece that commands and directs the viewer’s attention
Rhythm
Is use of repeated elements in an orderly yet non-uniform manner to create a sense movement throughout the piece’s composition.
Unity / Variety
is the balanced use of different shapes, colors, sizes, texture in art piece to add wholesomeness and interest. Variety and unity should work together to ensure that the piece is neither monotonous nor chaotic.
